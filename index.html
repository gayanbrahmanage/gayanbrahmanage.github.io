University of Calgary, Calgary, Canada.

Calgary, Canada. Developed a real-time 3D-SLAM software package using C++ and GTK for Linux environments, integrating advanced findings from my PhD research with current literature. The software features a multi-threaded architecture with dedicated threads for depth prediction, local mapping, sparse bundle adjustment, and loop closing, operating alongside main threads for GUI visualization. It employs a parallel processing structure for loop processing, including key-point detection, matching, and initial motion estimation using RANSAC and EM, ensuring efficient and effective processing. Dynamic resource allocation optimizes thread performance based on available computational resources, significantly advancing the field of real-time 3D-SLAM technology. (Project page)

Skoog Music Instrument - R&D

Power Migration Partners UK Ltd

Jan 2014 - Mar 2014

Under the supervision of Power Migration Partners Ltd. in the United Kingdom, I conducted research and development on a joystick for the Skoog Music instrument. This project focused on understanding and enhancing the performance of a foam joystick equipped with a 3-axis magnetic sensor.

My responsibilities included researching the output patterns of the magnetic sensors based on the pressure applied to the foam joystick. I analyzed the noise patterns and identified distinct patterns for both individual sensors and combinations of sensors under varying conditions. I applied advanced filtering techniques to refine sensor data and developed an algorithm to accurately estimate the point of pressure application on the joystick.

To achieve this, I created a multivariate Gaussian model in n-dimensional space to calculate the likelihood of template-to-measurement matching, based on consistent patterns observed during the calibration stage. This model significantly improved the joystick's accuracy and responsiveness, enhancing the overall user experience of the Skoog Music instrument.

This project allowed me to apply my expertise in sensor data analysis, algorithm development, and signal processing, contributing to innovative advancements in musical technology.
